IRS · Online Account concept

Omni bar — in the logged-in account

A single smart search bar on the Online Account (IOLA) home. It understands plain language, notice codes, and dollar amounts, answers inline, and surfaces top IRS.gov tasks that aren't in IOLA yet — clicking one opens the IRS.gov page in a new tab. Interactive prototype, walkthrough PDFs, and screenshots are all in this folder.

1 · Omni bar on the account home

The classic Online Account layout with the omni bar on top. Try typing “can't pay”, “CP523”, “pay 200”, or “start a business”. Tip: press 1–4 / ← → to switch between sample taxpayers.

2 · Modern variant — omnibar in the header

The same smart search, but moved into the masthead as a command palette — the pattern from Microsoft Teams, Chrome, and GitHub. A compact pill sits in the header on every tab; focusing it (or pressing / or ⌘K) turns it white, dims the page behind a scrim, and drops the results panel right under the bar. Same engine: plain language, notice codes, dollar amounts, and featured non-IOLA tasks.

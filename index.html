Online Account — Prototypes

Prototypes

Design prototypes and concept explorations for IRS Taxpayer Services & Online Accounts. Styled to the IRS Online Design Guidelines (ODG) / U.S. Web Design System.

New

Issue #7 · Variant 3

Payer dashboard — identity-first quick-pay

A returning payer's home: a history of every payment made on others' behalf, plus a single-screen quick-pay panel organized around the verification-tier ladder — your identity tier decides whether a payment posts instantly and lands in your history.

 New

Issue #7 · Variant 2

Bulk submission — institution console

A bank or business paying for many taxpayers at once: CSV upload → validated review grid (ready vs. error rows) → one-declaration batch submit. The institution's identity-proofed identity is bound to every payment as the payer of record.

 New

Issue #7 · Variant 1

Pay on behalf — guided wizard

A step-by-step third-party payment flow: pick payer type, capture the payment reason (notice/form), identify the taxpayer & obligation, set amount + application instructions, then capture payer-type-aware identity. Verification tiers gate submission (limits + immediate vs. flagged posting) and the receipt spells out the payer of record.

 New

Unified Tax Account

One login, pick your role

Sign in once, then act as an individual, a business you run or belong to, or a tax pro — with the omni bar integrated everywhere and re-scoping itself to whichever account you switch into.

 New

Refunds & Returns

Where's My Refund? — timeline tracker

A delivery-style status tracker that shows every state a refund or amended return has moved through — plus what's expected next — instead of just three vague stages.

 New

Help & Support

Taxpayer Assistant (AI agent)

A live chat AI agent that uses tools — it looks up your account, fills out forms (8822, 2848), and faxes them to the right unit, narrating each tool call.

 New

Business Tax Account

94x Online Signature PIN

Self-service wizard for a responsible official to request the 10-digit 94x PIN (Forms 940/941/943/944/945) and e-file straight to MeF.
